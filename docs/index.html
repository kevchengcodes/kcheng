ABOUT ME

My curiosity naturally led me to engineering; my fascination with aerospace and sustainable energy led me to MechE. But MechE wasn't the only thing I was interested in. I knew I wanted to learn more about how to run a company. And how to code. And how to communicate my ideas effectively. That's how I ended up at Northwestern: it gave me the community to grow and the flexibility to expand. From here, I started to learn how management thinks with my Certificate in Managerial Analytics from the business school. I started to code both within my curriculum and from designing webpages for Dance Marathon. And I became who I am today through my friends, my struggles, and my accomplishments.

Other facts about me... I love playing soccer, piano, and going on hikes. I stay as active as possible in the community and organized numerous philanthropies benefitting St. Jude Children's Research Hospital and the Evanston Animal Shelter. I also participated and helped put together Northwestern's annual 30 Hour Dance Marathon from 2013-2016, serving as part of the website and finance committees. Over that 4 year span, I am proud to say that we raised over $4.5 million for our beneficiaries! I volunteered constantly in the Evanston area at places such as The Campus Kitchens Project, which worked to pack and deliver food to the underprivileged families in the surrounding area, and The Talking Farm, an urban farm aimed at teaching young children about urban farming and sustainability. Wherever I end up, I look forward to continuing my engagement with the community around me.
